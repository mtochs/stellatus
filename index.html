Autonomous Solutions for a Safer, More Productive Mine

Real-Time Ore Face Characterization

Problem: Ore/waste misclassification is a massive blind spot, leading to lost revenue when valuable ore is dumped as waste and increased costs when worthless rock is milled.

Solution: Our autonomous drone platform scans the post-blast rock face to generate a near real-time 'grade map,' providing shovel operators with precise, data-driven instructions on exactly what to dig.

  • Maximize recovered grade and minimize ore loss.
  • Reduce dilution to lower energy and water consumption per tonne.
  • Create an auditable, digital record of every blast face.

Predictive Maintenance for Conveyor Idlers

Problem: A single failed conveyor idler—a low-cost part—can rip a multi-million-dollar belt, causing catastrophic unplanned downtime. Manually inspecting thousands of these components is dangerous, inefficient, and often ineffective.

Solution: An autonomous ground robot patrols conveyor lines 24/7, using a suite of thermal and acoustic sensors to predict which specific idlers are degrading and will fail—before they cause a shutdown.

  • Eliminate idler-induced downtime and prevent catastrophic belt loss.
  • Shift from reactive repairs to planned, predictive maintenance.
  • Improve worker safety by removing technicians from hazardous environments.

Autonomous Tailings Dam Integrity Monitoring

Problem: Meeting the August 2025 Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management (GISTM) is a non-negotiable mandate. Current manual monitoring is infrequent and cannot reliably predict the subtle changes that precede a catastrophic failure.

Solution: We provide an AI-powered platform that uses autonomous drones to create a 'digital twin' of your tailings facilities, continuously analyzing multi-sensor data to predict geotechnical instability before it happens.

  • Achieve provable, auditable GISTM conformance.
  • Enhance safety and ESG performance with a proactive system.
  • Transform risk management from a descriptive to a predictive model.

Autonomous Stope & Void Mapping

Problem: After blasting, the true shape of an underground stope is an unknown black hole, leading directly to lost ore and dilution. Sending surveyors to the edge of these unstable voids is one of the most hazardous jobs in a mine.

Solution: A fully autonomous drone flies into hazardous, GPS-denied stopes to create a complete and accurate 3D map in minutes, providing critical data for reconciliation and planning with zero human exposure to risk.

  • Maximize ore recovery and reduce dilution by optimizing future blasts.
  • Eliminate one of the most dangerous tasks in underground mining.
  • Provide rapid, accurate data for geotechnical and planning teams.
