Our Mission

Stellatus was founded with a vision to revolutionize space safety and autonomy. As humanity's presence in space expands, so do the challenges of operating in this increasingly congested environment.

Our team of aerospace engineers, AI specialists, and orbital mechanics experts are dedicated to developing cutting-edge solutions that enhance satellite resilience and autonomy in the face of emerging threats.

The Challenge

34,000+ Trackable Debris Objects
1,500+ Conjunction Alerts Per Satellite Per Year

Modern satellites operate in an increasingly congested, contested, and complex orbital environment. With over 34,000 trackable debris objects and an average of 1,500+ new conjunction alerts generated per satellite per year (e.g. Starlink, Planet), operators are overwhelmed, and response latency is high.

Even more critically, current defensive postures against ASAT threats or orbital breakups depend heavily on ground-based detection and response loops—which are vulnerable to disruption and far too slow for real-time threat environments.

Our Solution

We are developing the first onboard AI system capable of real-time threat assessment and autonomous maneuver planning. This will enable satellites to react instantly to hostile behavior, debris-generating events, or uncoordinated maneuvers—without waiting for Earth-based instructions.

Our system serves as a wedge product toward a broader "Lattice for Space" autonomy stack, unlocking a new class of resilient, intelligent, self-defending spacecraft.
